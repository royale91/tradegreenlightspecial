Module 05

GLS OSOB — Exits

GLS OSOB is the companion pane below price. Its one job is the exit — it tells you when a long is getting tired, when a short is getting tired, and rings the EXIT BELL so you scale or tighten instead of round-tripping a winner.

How to Read It

The OSOB Pane

  • ⚪ White wave drifting — let it ride.
  • 🔴 Red top (overbought) — long getting tired → scale out of longs.
  • 🟢 Green bottom (oversold) — short getting tired → scale out of shorts.
  • 🟡 GOLD dots = the EXIT BELL — extreme rolling over → take it / tighten.
  • ▲▼ zero-cross — momentum flipped.
⚠ Honest Framing

Exits Only — Never Entries

Overbought / oversold oscillators work in ranges and fail in trends — so OSOB is for exits and scaling only, never to fade a trend or pick an entry.

That's why it's a companion to GLS, not a standalone signal. When the Yellow Man lights up on price and OSOB rings the gold exit bell, the move is done — protect the profit and wait for the next clean setup.

The Method — Put It Together

GLS gets you in, OSOB gets you out. Run them in order, respect the firewall, and let the math do its job.

1

Direction

Trade with the stack — Day / 4h / 15m / 5m all pointing the same way.

2

Wait for SYSTEM GO

Only act on SYSTEM GO. No light, no trade — you sit on your hands.

3

Enter

Take the entry, stop, target and size that GLS draws on the chart for you.

4

Manage

Breakeven at +1R, then trail. The runner can't hurt you after that.

5

Use OSOB to exit

The gold EXIT BELL → take profit, scale out, or tighten the stop.

6

Respect the firewall

Stop at the daily goal or loss limit. Skip the chop. Live to trade tomorrow.

The rules it enforces: with-trend only · in-session only · above-average volume · never chop · never chase · always a stop + daily limit.
