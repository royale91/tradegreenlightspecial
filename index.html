The 3-Step ORB

Opening-Range Breakout

The fakeout filter is the whole point. Mark the range, wait for the break, and let the retest prove it before you go.

1

Mark the range

Auto-marks the high and low of the first 15 minutes — the gray ORB lines that define the day's battleground.

2

Wait for the breakout

A body close beyond the range, in the trend direction. No close, no trade — you're not guessing the break.

3

Retest → confirm → GO

Price returns, holds, and closes back in trend = the GO. A close back inside prints FAKE — don't chase.

Read the Tank

The OSOB & the Yellow Man

Two tools that tell you when a move is running out of gas — so you bank profit and stand aside instead of giving it all back.

GLS OSOB

The OSOB Tank

The lower pane reads how stretched momentum is. Spike into the red top and the move is overbought — start scaling out of longs. Flush into the green bottom and it's oversold — scale out of shorts. It keeps you taking money out at the extremes instead of round-tripping a winner.

  • Red top — overbought · trim & take profit on longs
  • Green bottom — oversold · trim & take profit on shorts
  • Gold dots = the EXIT BELL — momentum is spent, the ride is over
  • ▲▼ — a momentum flip; the tide is turning
⚠ Yellow Man

The Yellow Man Warning

The Yellow Man candle is your early-warning light — it fires the instant the trend starts to turn. It's a heads-up to tighten your stop and get ready, never a trade by itself.

When the Yellow Man lights up and the OSOB rings the exit bell, the move is done — protect the profit and wait for the next clean setup. Discipline over hope, every time.
